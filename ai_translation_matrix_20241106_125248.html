AI Technical Language Translation Matrix
  Instead of Saying... Say...
0 Hyperparameter Tuning Finding the right combination of settings for an AI model to work well, like adjusting the focus and brightness on a camera to get the perfect shot.
1 Transfer Learning Using an AI model that's already learned to recognize certain patterns to help another AI model learn faster and more accurately, like using a map to navigate an unfamiliar city.
2 Natural Language Processing (NLP) Helping computers understand and process human language, like how Siri or Google Assistant can understand what you say and respond accordingly.
3 Reinforcement Learning Teaching an AI model to make decisions by giving it feedback in the form of rewards or penalties, like training a dog to fetch by giving it treats when it does something right.
4 Overfitting When an AI model is too good at memorizing the training data and can't generalize to new, unseen data, like a student who only learns the answers to the questions on the test instead of understanding the material.
5 Edge Computing Processing data and making decisions closer to where the data is generated, like analyzing security footage on a local camera instead of sending it to a remote server.
6 Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) A type of AI model that's great at processing sequential data, like speech or text, by looking at the context of each piece of information.
7 Gradient Descent An optimization algorithm that helps an AI model find the best solution by iteratively adjusting its parameters based on the difference between its predictions and the actual results, like a compass helping a hiker find their way.
8 Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) A type of AI model that's great at recognizing patterns in images, like faces or objects, by analyzing small regions of the image at a time.
9 Ensembling Combining the predictions of multiple AI models to make a more accurate prediction, like averaging the grades of multiple teachers to get a better sense of a student's abilities.
10 Autoencoders A type of AI model that's great at compressing and reconstructing data, like compressing a video to make it easier to store and then reconstructing it without losing any quality.
11 Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) A type of AI model that learns to generate new data that's similar to existing data, like generating new images of faces that look like they could be real.
12 Baseline Model A simple AI model that serves as a reference point to measure the performance of other more complex models, like a benchmark to compare how well different cars perform.
13 Active Learning A process where an AI model is actively involved in selecting the most informative data to learn from, like a student choosing which questions to ask their teacher to clarify their doubts.
14 Explainable AI (XAI) A set of techniques to help users understand and trust the decisions made by an AI model, like showing a doctor how a medical diagnosis was made using patient data.
